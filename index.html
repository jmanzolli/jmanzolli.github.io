Selected impact

Three research contributions

Systems that connect electric mobility, intelligent decision-making, and the power grid — each grounded in a real operational result.

Electric bus operations · cold climates

Planning resilient electric bus fleets in cold regions

+30% winter energy demand

Problem
In cold winters, electric buses lose range and cost more to run, and the local grid can only deliver so much power.
Contribution
An agent-based simulation of a whole bus fleet in winter, coupled to an optimization model that plans vehicles and charging around it.
Role
Lead author — built the model and ran the analysis.
View paper · Applied Energy Code

V2G · battery-aware fleet charging

Robust charging that accounts for battery ageing and uncertainty

−37% operating cost · −12% under the robust model

Problem
Charging a bus fleet is costly, and doing it carelessly strains the grid and wears the batteries out faster.
Contribution
A charging strategy that reacts to changing tariffs and battery wear, holds up under uncertain daily use, and can sell power back to the grid.
Role
Lead author — built the model and case study.
View paper · Applied Energy Code

Autonomous EVs · grid resilience

Shared autonomous EVs as backup power during outages

28% of local energy needs supplied

Problem
Storms and outages often knock out power and public transport at the same time — could one fleet help with both?
Contribution
A spatial optimization framework, tested on Montreal, for fleets that move people and act as backup power during outages.
Role
Lead author — built the optimization framework.
View paper · npj Sustainable Mobility

About

Research that connects EVs, power systems, and decision-making

My work develops quantitative and computational methods for electric vehicles and energy systems, with particular emphasis on smart charging, vehicle-to-grid services, aggregators, battery degradation, energy markets, and grid-aware operation.

I build models that are rigorous enough to defend in review and concrete enough to hand to an operator — which is why most of my research ships with code, and why the strongest results have become software.

I hold a PhD in Sustainable Energy Systems from the University of Coimbra, completed summa cum laude, an MSc in Energy for Sustainability, and a BSc in Electrical Engineering from the University of Campinas, including an exchange program at the Technical University of Munich.
